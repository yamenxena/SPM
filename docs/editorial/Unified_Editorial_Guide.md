## 1. General Style

- Retain the simple narrative-report style; stay close to the original, remove repetition.
  - **Example:** "Some people would hallucinate and see imaginary animals" → **"some people would hallucinate"**
- Avoid lengthy wording in titles or headings.
- Written texts slightly more formal than subtitles ("Children" in profiles; "Kids" in subtitles).
- Avoid "get" in formal text. Fine in subtitles.
- Use "base" not "headquarters" — only the top-level facility is HQ.
  - **Example:** The Hisba Diwan headquarters in Raqqa was the Muawiyah School Prison. All other buildings were bases.
- Avoid "hall" except for very large rooms.
- American English throughout (neighborhood, tire). Exception: **British English for Sednaya floors** (ground floor, first floor, second floor…) — witnesses use these terms in testimonies; Arabic translates directly to British naming.
- Remove bold introduction paragraphs from profiles if present.
- Prefer "remain unknown" over "remain shrouded in mystery" or similar embellishments.

## 2. Transliteration & Spelling

Transliterate according to **Fus'ha conventions.** Cross-reference with Google Maps; exercise discretion for well-known English spellings (e.g. Sulaymaniyah).

### Rules

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Remove "al-" from place names and concepts. Keep for people and compound names. | Raqqa (not Ar-Raqqa), Bukamal (not Al-Bukamal), Meydan Prison (not Al-Meydan). But: Deir ez-Zor, Sahwet al-Khudr |
| Do not capitalize "al-" unless at start of sentence. | "They detained al-Abdullah." / "Al-Abdullah was detained." |
| **يْ** between consonants → "ei" | al-Oweid (note: we didn't do this with Rumaila) |
| **ء** and **ع** → "a" | Shafa |
| **ة** → "a" | Salamiya, Hisba (not Hisbah), Raqqa (not Raqqah). Exception: well-known spellings with "h" (Sulaymaniyah) |
| Long **ي** → "i" | Mayadin |
| Long **و** → "ou" | Baloum |
| Apostrophes sparingly, only if common | Qa'im |
| American English spelling | Neighborhood, Tire |
| **Once-then-English:** Use the English translation on first mention with Arabic transliteration in brackets. Use English equivalent only for all subsequent occurrences. | First: Tire [doulab]. After: Tire |
| **No quotation marks around names.** Arabic quotes names; English does not. | ✅ Um al-Mauuna Church ❌ "Um al-Mauuna Church" |

## 6. Grammar & Punctuation

1. **Capitalize** first letter of each word in headings. *The Chameleon: The Testimony of an ISIS Jailer*
2. **Oxford commas.** A lack of space, food, and water.
3. Numbers 1–9 spelled out; 10+ as numerals. Exception: Block 4, Cell 2 in subtitles (must be consistent).
4. Punctuation **inside** quotation marks. "15 cartons of cigarettes."
5. **American date format.** June 29 (not 29 June). Written text: August 8 2021. Avoid 8/8/2021 except in documents/diagrams.
6. **Double quotation marks** for speech: "They arrested me early one morning," he says.
7. **Single quotation marks** for speech within speech: "It sounded like 'twgl' or something."
8. **Double quotation marks** for highlighted words: accused of "terrorism."
9. **Square brackets** for editorial additions: "They accused us of speaking with the [Global] Coalition."
10. **Parentheses** for additional information.
11. Lower case after colon.
12. Unspaced, pointed initials: R.J.
13. Spaced dash for interjections: The word *taazir* – meaning "discretionary punishment" – is a common term.
14. 🔴 Do NOT use em-dashes (---) at all.
15. ⚠️ **No semicolons** in body text. Keep sentences short and simple — no complex linking with while/so/semicolons.
15. Title and subtitle joined by colon, each word capitalized.
15. Do NOT put quotation marks around names or noms de guerre.

### Specific Rules

- "detained **on** charges of…" | "smuggle something **out** of…"
- Don't capitalize "prison" unless named (Sednaya Prison vs. "in prison"). Capitalize "Coalition" (anti-ISIS).
- ISIS arrests = "arrest" or "abduction" — "kidnapping" only if ransom involved.
- Only transliterate Arabic-origin terms. Never italicize native English, military ranks, or "tuberculosis."
- *sharia* (lowercase, italicized). Italicize rare Arabic words: *muhtasib*, *iftar*, *maghreb*, *sufra*, *imam*, *mahram*, *sharaqa*, *bouriya*, *tasyif*, *arsa*, *bukhshe*, *samoon*, *suhoor*, *gellabiyya*, *najis*, *izar*, *takbirat*, *Murjia*, *dayyuth*, *hudud*. But: **Sunni, Shia, Ramadan** — NOT italicized.
- Do NOT put quotation marks around names, church names, or noms de guerre.
- Do NOT put Latin-origin brand/product names in quotation marks (e.g. Kiri, Starex).
- 🔴 Do NOT put quotation marks around museum names (ISIS Prisons Museum, Syria Prisons Museum).
- "charges brought against them" (not just "charges").
- "ISIS videos" (not "video clips" or "video productions").
- 🔴 Avoid "file" for investigations. Use "investigation" for large topics (e.g. the Shaitat Massacre); "reports" for sub-parts within.
- 🔴 "in the prison" (not "inside the prison").
- "up/down the stairs" (not "through the stairs"). If unclear direction: "by the stairs."
- "according to instructions" (not "as per instructions").
- "remain unknown" (not "remain shrouded in mystery").
- After "He said," always include comma before quote: He said, "…"
- **Past tense** for Assad regime institutions. **Present tense** for video testimonies: "He says… he recalls…"
- "base" not "headquarters" — only the top-level facility is HQ.
- American English for Sednaya floors: **British English exception** — basement, ground floor, first floor, second floor, third floor, fourth floor. Witnesses use these terms; Arabic translates directly to British naming.

## 7. Subtitles

- Same number of lines in Arabic and English. Never merge or split lines.
- Use periods (.) to end sentences; all other punctuation sparingly.
- Bracket glosses on first usage: *23 Shabat (February 23) Street in Raqqa*
- No quotation marks around names or noms de guerre.
- No colon before speech. Commas for reported speech.
- Use "interviewer" (not "journalist") — only when not obvious from context.
  - Format: `Interviewer:` / `Witness:` (capital I/W).
- Capitalize only at start of new sentences; lower case for continuations.
- Numbers >9 as numerals.
- In subtitles: capitalize first letter of direct speech.
- Use "base" rather than "headquarters."

## 8. Citations

Chicago Style Guide (17th edition):
1. Full name(s): Lastname, Firstname Middlename
2. "Article Title" (quotation marks, major words capitalized)
3. Column name (if applicable, major words capitalized)
4. *Publication Name* (italicized, major words capitalized)
5. Publication date (Month Day, Year)
6. URL (for online)
7. "Last accessed…"

Footnote number goes **after** the comma or full stop. Footnotes end with a period.

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT Instructions
> Source: Robin's editorial directives (WhatsApp voice messages, May 2025 — Jan 2026). Each item marked ⚠️ is a Robin-confirmed rule.

### Terminology

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| ⚠️ Never use "terrorism" generically | It is a meaningless term in this context. Avoid or contextualize with specifics. |
| ⚠️ Never "leaked documents" | The regime fell; documents were found/recovered, not leaked. Use "documents" only. |
| ⚠️ بيروقراطية القتل = **bureaucracy of murder** | Never "killing bureaucracy" or "bureaucracy of killing". |

| ⚠️ حاجز = **checkpoint** | Not "outpost". |

### Tense Rules (Expanded)

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| ⚠️ **Building = present tense** | Prison buildings still exist → "The hallway has high windows that provide the only light." |
| ⚠️ **Events = past tense** | What happened to prisoners → "Prisoners were assigned to cells and subjected to torture." |
| ⚠️ **Reporting verbs = present tense** | "The witness says… he recalls… she describes…" — always present, even when quoting past events. |
| ⚠️ **Closed institutions = past tense** | If institution ceased operations, all operational verbs must be past. Even when source uses present tense with specific dates, convert to past. |

### Style

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| ⚠️ **Arabic transliteration pattern** | Introduce Arabic term once with English gloss: *shabeh* (suspension). Then use only English from then on. Do not repeat Arabic each time. |

### Names & Consistency

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| ⚠️ **Consistent name spelling is legally critical** | If cases go to court, inconsistent name spelling across documents weakens evidence. Lawyers will exploit inconsistencies. |
| ⚠️ **Yousuf** = Y-O-U-S-U-F | Never Youssef, Yusef, or Yusuf. |
| ⚠️ **Master name list** | Always cross-reference the master name list before translating any document with witnesses or named individuals. |

### Process

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| ⚠️ **Consolidate documents** | All items for one page/investigation should be on the translation teams sheet in one place. |
| ⚠️ **Corrections are forward-looking** | No need to go back and fix already-published documents unless critical. Robin fixes as he goes. |
